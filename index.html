Causative Factors of Road Traffic Crashes in Lagos Road traffic crashes is caused by a number of reasons. Sometimes it is caused as a result of absence of effective transport planning, vehicle-misuse, poor management, inadequate street parking, traffic congestion, delays and accidents among other contributory factors. Road Traffic Accidents are a major cause of morbidity and mortality worldwide, but especially in low-and middle-income countries. The World Health Organization estimates that more than 3000 people are killed every day in road traffic accidents globally, with at least 30,000 others injured or disabled. According to National Bureau of Statistics, 53.2% of road crashes in Lagos state in 2018 was caused by Speed Violation. An indication that high number of vehicle drivers violates the prescribed speed rate. A call to action to further enforce the law. Road Traffic Accidents in Ikeja, Lagos Island, Lagos Mainland and Ajeromi Ifelodun. A deeper look into the traffic accidents that occurred in the four major local government in Lagos State indicates that the rate of accidents increases as the population in each local government increases. The increase in population directly leads to an increase in the number of pedestrians on the road. The fact that this affects accidents rate may be an indication that there is little or no infrastructure that caters for pedestrians on the road and in fact maybe they are exposed to roads when walking by or crossing. Lagos Island as shown in the graph has the highest number of traffic crashes over the years while Ajeromi Ifelodun has the lowest number of traffic crashes.
